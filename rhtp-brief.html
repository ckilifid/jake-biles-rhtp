The single most important node: Big Sky Care Connect.
Every Initiative 5.1 technology dollar flows through or alongside it.
Montana RHTP Ecology Map — Companion Reference One node per row. Follow the whiteboard left-to-right, top-to-bottom.
  1. Left column — the setup CMS releases $233M to Montana in Year One — part of a $50B national Rural Health Transformation Program. The money is real and it's moving.
  2. Governor Gianforte and DPHHS built the application. Montana ranked fourth-highest allocation of any state.
  3. Senator Sheehy isn't the allocator — but his two bills (Critical Access for Veterans Care Act + CONNECT for Health Act) are routing more patients and more data to the exact rural facilities this money is modernizing.
  4. Center column — the gate DPHHS is the allocator. Five initiatives. The one that matters here: Initiative 5 — Technology Innovation.
  5. Initiative 5 splits into two tracks: EHR modernization (hospitals buying Epic/Cerner) and data usability (making the data actionable at the point of care). CAPTN's lane is the second one.
  6. Big Sky Care Connect (BSCC) is the state-designated Health Information Exchange. DPHHS is finalizing a Statement of Work with BSCC right now — Q2 2026. Every Initiative 5.1 dollar flows through or alongside it. This is the gate.
  7. Initiative 5.1 is explicitly calling for "provider-facing tools to translate analytics into actionable insights at the point of care." A behavioral health bed registry is the named near-term use case. That description is CAPTN's decisioning intelligence layer.
  8. CAPTN's play: not selling a procurement platform to the state, not competing with EHR vendors. Positioning as the intelligence layer on top of BSCC's HIE data — making the state's massive EHR investment actually useful at the point of care.
  9. Right column — the credentialing orbit Next Frontier Capital, Two Bear Capital, and Frontier Angels are the Montana VC firms most likely to have portfolio companies competing for Initiative 5.1 dollars. The play for CAPTN isn't investment — it's local relationship access and credentialing.
  10. The clock ~8 competitive RFPs by September 2026. The window to position alongside BSCC is open now and closes fast.
What to Test
  • Has CAPTN been in conversation with Big Sky Care Connect? The Q2 2026 SOW is finalizing now.
  • Is DPHHS open to a third-party decisioning layer on top of the HIE, or are they building it in-house?
  • eMACS is being fully replaced. RFP closed February 24, 2026. The state called it "a bottleneck for efficient government operations." CAPTN may have a positioning opportunity as the intelligence layer during that procurement system transition.
